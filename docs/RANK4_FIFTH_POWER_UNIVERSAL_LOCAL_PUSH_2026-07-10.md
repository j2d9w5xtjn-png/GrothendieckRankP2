## 2. The fifth-power identity

The regular-translation recurrence is

\[
 P_{r+4}-\alpha P_{r+2}+\delta P_r=0.
\]

At (r=-1) it gives

\[
 P_3=\alpha\operatorname {Id}-\delta S.
\]

At (r=1), using (delta^2=1),

\[
\begin{aligned}
 P_5
 &=\alpha P_3-\delta\operatorname {Id}\\
 &=(2\alpha-\delta)\operatorname {Id}-\alpha S\\
 &=\operatorname {Id}+\alpha(\operatorname {Id}-S).
\end{aligned}
\]

Put

\[
 E=P_5-\operatorname {Id}.
\]

The ideal generated by (E(A)) is exactly (M=alpha J).

## 3. Structure forced on the fifth-power deviation

### Proposition 3.1

The following identities hold:

\[
 M^2=0,qquad JM=0,qquad 2M=0,qquad \alpha M=0,
\]

and

\[
 E(ab)=aE(b)+bE(a),qquad E^2=0,qquad E(J)=0.
\]

#### Proof

The first line follows from
(alpha J^2=0), (2alpha J=0), and
(alpha^2=2alpha).  In particular (E(a)E(b)=0).  Since (P_5) is an
algebra map,

\[
 E(ab)=aE(b)+bE(a)+E(a)E(b)
       =aE(b)+bE(a).
\]

The exponent-eight theorem gives

\[
 P_5^2=P_{25}=P_1,
\]

because (25\equiv1\pmod8).  Thus (2E+E^2=0); both terms vanish because
(2E=0).

Also

\[
 E\circ q=P_{10}-P_2=0.
\]

Therefore (E) kills every generator ((q-e)(a)) of (J).  If
(j=\sum c_i(q-e)(a_i)), the derivation identity gives

\[
 E(j)=\sum (q-e)(a_i)E(c_i)=0
\]

because (JM=0).  This proves (E(J)=0).  (square)

### Corollary 3.2 (Kähler obstruction)

Let (B=A/J).  The map (E) descends to an (R)-derivation

\[
 \bar E:B\longrightarrow M,
\]

where the (A)-action on (M) factors through (B).  Since (M) is the
ideal generated by (E(A)), the induced (B)-linear map

\[
 \Omega_{B/R}\longrightarrow M
\]

is surjective.

Moreover (E(alpha)=0), since (P_5(delta)=delta^5=delta), and
(E(chi)=0), since (S(chi)=chi).  Hence the surjection factors as

\[
 \boxed{
 \Omega_{B/R}/(d\bar\alpha,d\bar\chi)\twoheadrightarrow M.}
\]

In particular the rank-four conjecture holds whenever the displayed quotient
of differentials vanishes.  This contains, for example, the formally
unramified 2-torsion-equalizer case, but it does not settle the local--local
fibers where the differential module is large.

### Proposition 3.3 (the precise Frobenius statement)

Let (lambda:A\to R) be any Frobenius functional and put
(B_lambda(a,b)=lambda(ab)).  There is a unique unit (j_sigma\in A^\times)
such that

\[
 \lambda(\sigma(a))=\lambda(j_\sigma a),\qquad \sigma=P_5.
\]

Writing (theta=j_sigma-1), one has

\[
 j_\sigma\sigma(j_\sigma)=1,\qquad
 B_\lambda(Ea,b)+B_\lambda(a,Eb)=B_\lambda(\theta a,b),
\]

and, independently of (theta),

\[
 B_\lambda(Ea,Eb)=0.
\]

#### Proof

The first assertion is Frobenius duality applied to (lambda circ sigma).
Applying the defining identity twice and using (sigma^2=1) gives
(j_sigma sigma(j_sigma)=1).  Since (E) is a derivation,

\[
 B_\lambda(Ea,b)+B_\lambda(a,Eb)
 =\lambda(E(ab))
 =\lambda(\sigma(ab))-\lambda(ab)
 =B_\lambda(\theta a,b).
\]

Finally (E(a)E(b)=0), because (E(A) is contained in M) and (M^2=0).
(square)

It is important that no proof of (theta=0) is known.  A tempting convolution
argument for (lambda circ E=0) is invalid: (D=P_4-e) is an (A)-valued map,
so it cannot be pulled through the scalar functional (lambda).  Thus the
fifth-power package gives total isotropy of the image but not skew-adjointness
for a chosen Frobenius form.  The missing input is still that (E) comes
simultaneously from the square word, the determinant character, and one
self-universal Hopf translation matrix.

## 4. Why the soft fifth-power package is insufficient

Let

\[
 R=\mathbf F_2[r]/r^2,qquad A=R[t]/t^4,qquad
 \delta=1+t^2,qquad S(t)=(1+r)t.
\]

Then (delta^2=1), (S^2=1), and

\[
 J=((\operatorname {Id}-S)(A))=(rt),qquad
 (1+delta)J=(rt^3)\ne0.
\]

The map

\[
 \sigma=\operatorname {Id}+(1+delta)(\operatorname {Id}-S),qquad
 \sigma(t)=t+rt^3,
\]

is a nontrivial involutive augmented algebra automorphism.  Its deviation is
a square-zero derivation, it is identity on the cotangent space, and in this
example it even preserves the standard Frobenius functional extracting the
coefficient of (t^3).  It is **not** the fifth-power map of a Hopf structure.
Thus the abstract involution, derivation, and conormal properties, even with
preservation of a Frobenius functional imposed separately, cannot prove
(M=0).

There is a stronger calibration which also satisfies all of the algebra-level
(q,U,z)-identities.  Over

\[
 R=\mathbf F_2[\pi]/(\pi^8),\qquad
 A=R[T]/(T^4-\pi^4T^3),
\]

put

\[
 q(T)=\pi T^2,\qquad z=\pi^2T^2,\qquad
 S(T)=T+\pi T^2+\pi^2T^3.
\]

Then (S) is an involutive augmented algebra automorphism and, with
(U=Id-S),

\[
 z^2=qz=Uz=0,\qquad q^2=zq=qU=Uq,\qquad
 J_q=J_U=(\pi T^2,\pi T^3).
\]

Moreover (sigma=Id+zU) sends (T) to (T+\pi^7T^3), so its defect is nonzero.
The dependency-free checker

    scripts/audit_p5_strong_soft_countermodel_20260710.py

audits the quotient algebra, all displayed maps and identities, equality of
the two deviation ideals, and the nonzero tight witness.

This model cannot carry a compatible (t^4) Hopf coproduct.  Its first
quartic-relation symbol is (T^3), whereas primitive lifting would force that
symbol to be primitive, and (T^3) is primitive in neither geometric (t^4)
fiber.  The exact checker

    scripts/audit_p5_fake_hopf_realizability_20260710.py

confirms this directly: for both (c_4=0,1), relation stability and the exact
condition (mu Delta(T)=pi T^2) are already UNSAT even when coassociativity is
omitted.  The two audited commands end in

    c4 0 unsat
    c4 1 unsat

This identifies Hopf relation stability, rather than another soft word-map
identity, as a load-bearing input.

An independent matrix attack reached the same boundary: orthogonality of the
translation matrix, determinant square one, the integral-trace identity, and
the factored characteristic polynomial admit an abstract tight-chain model.
The dependency-free exact checker

    scripts/audit_rt_orthogonal_countermodel_20260710.py

verifies such a matrix with nonzero ((delta-1)(T^2-I)) over a coefficient
algebra of rank eight.  Thus only the fact that the coefficient algebra is
itself the rank-four Hopf algebra remains capable of excluding it.  One small
positive consequence survives: in characteristic two, if (B) is a perfect
symmetric form and (a_B=(B(v,v))) is its nonalternation ideal, Pfaffian
functoriality gives

\[
 \det(T)-1\in a_BA.
\]

For a Frobenius form this only yields ((1+delta)J is contained in a_BAJ),
and the rank-eight countermodel shows that this abstract bound is sharp.

## 5. The (t^4) gauge reduction

On (H=k[t]/t^4), write (u\circ v=u\otimes v+v\otimes u).  The pinned
killed-by-two coproducts have

\[
\begin{aligned}
 w(t)&=c_1(t\circ t^2)+c_1^2(t^2\circ t^3)
       +c_4t^2\otimes t^2,\\
 w(t^3)&=t\circ t^2+c_1(t^2\circ t^3).
\end{aligned}
\]

Set (t'=t+c_1t^3).  This augmented algebra automorphism is self-inverse and

\[
 (t')^2=t^2,qquad (t')^3=t^3.
\]

Therefore

\[
\begin{aligned}
 w(t')
 &=w(t)+c_1w(t^3)\\
 &=c_4t^2\otimes t^2
  =c_4(t')^2\otimes(t')^2.
\end{aligned}
\]

Thus ((c_1,c_4)\simeq(0,c_4)) over the original field.  Scaling
(u=at') sends (c_4) to (c_4/a^3).  After a faithfully flat extension

## 6. The universal localized deformation chart

Fix one of the four geometric fibers and a lifted basis
(1,e_1,e_2,e_3), with (I=(e_1,e_2,e_3)).  A general commutative
augmented multiplication has 18 deformation coefficients:

\[
 e_ie_j=\sum_{r=1}^3m_{ij}^re_r,qquad 1\le i\le j\le3.
\]

A general counital coproduct has 27:

\[
 \Delta(e_i)=e_i\otimes1+1\otimes e_i
 +\sum_{j,k=1}^3c_i^{jk}e_j\otimes e_k.
\]

Let

\[
 P=\mathbf Z[\text{18 }m\text{-deviations},
             \text{27 }c\text{-deviations}],qquad
 \mathfrak q=(2,\text{all 45 variables}).
\]

Pinning the constant terms to a chosen special fiber and imposing

- associativity;
- multiplicativity of (Delta);
- coassociativity;

gives an ideal (K\subset P).  Unit, counit, commutativity of the coordinate
algebra, and augmentation compatibility are built into the normal form.  No
antipode variables are necessary: over a local ring, a lift of the closed
fiber's convolution inverse is invertible in the finite convolution algebra.

There are 189 safe nonzero equations in the integral source:

- 54 ordered associativity equations (24 minimally independent);
- 54 reduced compatibility equations;
- 81 reduced coassociativity equations.

The redundant equations do not alter (K).  Form

\[
 \Phi_i^r=[2]^\#(e_i)_r,qquad
 T_i^r=\sum_s\Phi_i^s\Phi_s^r.
\]

The nine (T_i^r) are exactly the augmentation coordinates of
([4]^\#(e_i)).  The full local--local rank-four conjecture is equivalent to

\[
 T_i^r\in K_{\mathfrak q}
\]

for all nine targets in the four charts.

### Exact syzygy criterion

For one target (T), form the global polynomial row

\[
 F=[T\mid f_1\mid\cdots\mid f_{189}],
\]

where the (f_j) generate (K).  Then

\[
 T\in K_{\mathfrak q}
\]

if and only if the global syzygy module of (F) contains a column
((s,h_1,\ldots,h_{189})^{\mathsf t}) with (s\notin\mathfrak q).  Here this
means exactly that (s) has odd constant term.  The displayed column gives
the directly checkable identity

\[
 sT+\sum_jh_jf_j=0.
\]

Conversely, any local membership relation can be cleared of denominators to
give such a global syzygy.  This criterion avoids the two common errors:

- saturating by the maximal ideal, which is not localization at that ideal;
- using a local normal-form zero without exporting its hidden unit
  multiplier.

If a complete syzygy calculation has no generator with unit first
coefficient, then none exists, so the target is genuinely nonzero in the
universal local bialgebra.  That would be a counterexample seed, not a
timeout or a bounded-degree negative result.
