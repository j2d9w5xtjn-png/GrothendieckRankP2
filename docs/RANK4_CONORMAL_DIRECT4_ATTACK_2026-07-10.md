# A further rank-four attack: the 2-torsion conormal and a direct [4] search

**Date:** 2026-07-10  
**Status:** new hand reduction plus a bounded exact-computation design. This
note does **not** claim that every rank-four finite locally free group scheme
is killed by four, and it does not claim a counterexample.

## 1. Current conclusion

Let \(G/S\) be finite locally free of rank four and put
\(A=\mathcal O(G)\) on an affine chart. The strengthened
regular-translation argument in
REGULAR_TRANSLATION_RANK4_EXPONENT8_2026-07-10.md proves unconditionally that

\[
  [8]_G=e.
\]

It also proves that the fourth-power defect is killed by two. The desired
assertion \([4]_G=e\) remains open. The present pass makes two further
reductions:

1. Torti's 2026 theorem closes the only non-local--local noncommutative
   special fiber left in the old ledger, namely
   \(\alpha _2\rtimes\mu _2\). Thus a rank-four counterexample can be reduced
   to a local Artin base of residue characteristic two with local--local
   special fiber.
2. A new evaluation of the regular-translation polynomial shows that the
   entire fourth-power defect is a conormal class along the scheme of
   2-torsion elements. More precisely, the only missing identity is
   \((1+\delta)J=0\), while the rank-four polynomial already proves the next
   layer \((1+\delta)J^2=0\).

The two persistent stretched \(t^4_{11}\) timeout rows can therefore be
attacked by a direct \([4]\ne e\) query on only six Gorenstein socle lifts,
instead of repeating the much stronger and larger \(S'\)-failure query.

## 2. Notation from regular translation

Let

\[
  P_r=[r]^\#:A\longrightarrow A,\qquad
  q=P_2,\qquad p=P_4=q\circ q,\qquad
  e=\eta\varepsilon.
\]

Let \(S=P_{-1}\) be the antipode, let \(T\) be universal right translation,
and let \(\delta=\det(T)\in A\). The proved rank-four identities are

\[
  \delta^2=1,\qquad
  (T^2-\operatorname{Id})(T^2-\delta\operatorname{Id})=0,\qquad
  D:=p-e=(1+\delta)(q-e).
\]

For every integer \(r\), coefficientwise evaluation of the matrix identity
gives

\[
  P_{r+4}-(1+\delta)P_{r+2}+\delta P_r=0.       \tag{2.1}
\]

Only \(r=0,-2\) was needed for the exponent-16 theorem. The value \(r=-1\)
contains additional information.

## 3. The cubic-word identity

### Proposition 3.1

One has an identity of \(R\)-linear maps

\[
  \boxed{P_3=(1+\delta)\operatorname{Id}-\delta S.}       \tag{3.1}
\]

Consequently, for all \(a,b\in A\),

\[
  \boxed{(1+\delta)(a-Sa)(b-Sb)=0.}                       \tag{3.2}
\]

### Proof

Equation (3.1) is (2.1) with \(r=-1\). The map \(P_3\) is an algebra map,
because \(A\) is commutative and \(P_3=\mu^{(3)}\Delta^{(3)}\). The antipode
is also an algebra map on the commutative coordinate algebra. Put
\(\alpha=1+\delta\). Expanding \(P_3(ab)=P_3(a)P_3(b)\), and using
\(\delta^2=1\) and \(\alpha\delta=\alpha\), gives

\[
\begin{aligned}
0
 &=\bigl(\alpha a-\delta Sa\bigr)
   \bigl(\alpha b-\delta Sb\bigr)
   -\bigl(\alpha ab-\delta S(ab)\bigr)\\
 &=\alpha\bigl(ab-aSb-(Sa)b+(Sa)(Sb)\bigr)\\
 &=\alpha(a-Sa)(b-Sb).
\end{aligned}
\]

This is (3.2). \(\square\)

## 4. The exact conormal obstruction

Let \(J\subset A\) be the ideal generated by the values
\((\operatorname{Id}-S)(a)\), \(a\in A\). This is the ideal of the equalizer
of the identity and inverse maps. Equivalently it is the ideal of the closed
subscheme on which \(g^2=e\).

### Lemma 4.1

The ideal \(J\) is also generated by the values \((q-e)(a)\).

### Proof

For endomorphisms of \(A\), write convolution as
\(f*g=\mu(f\otimes g)\Delta\). The antipode identities and the word laws give

\[
  q-e=\operatorname{Id}*(\operatorname{Id}-S),\qquad
  \operatorname{Id}-S=(q-e)*S.
\]

The first formula puts every value of \(q-e\) in the ideal generated by the
values of \(\operatorname{Id}-S\); the second gives the reverse inclusion.
\(\square\)

### Corollary 4.2

One has

\[
  \boxed{(1+\delta)J^2=0,\qquad D(A)\subseteq(1+\delta)J.} \tag{4.1}
\]

The map \(D\) factors canonically through the conormal module:

\[
 A\xrightarrow{\ q-e\ }J/J^2
   \xrightarrow{\times(1+\delta)}(1+\delta)J.             \tag{4.2}
\]

Moreover,

\[
  \boxed{[4]_G=e\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad(1+\delta)J=0.}  \tag{4.3}
\]

### Proof

Equation (3.2) kills \(1+\delta\) times every product of two generators of
\(J\), hence \((1+\delta)J^2=0\). The remaining statements follow from
\(D=(1+\delta)(q-e)\) and the fact that the values of \(q-e\) generate \(J\).
\(\square\)

This sharpens the statement that \(D\) is a square-zero augmentation
derivation. It identifies the one layer on which a proof must act: the
conormal \(J/J^2\) of the 2-torsion equalizer. It also explains why scalar
trace and determinant identities can miss the tight chain. They kill the
quadratic layer, whereas a counterexample would be a nonzero linear class in
\((1+\delta)(J/J^2)\).

In equal characteristic two on a local--local fiber,
\(1+\delta=\delta-1\) and \((\delta-1)^2=0\). These nilpotence statements do
not by themselves imply \((\delta-1)J=0\); the explicit determinant example
in the exponent-16 report shows why setting \(\delta=1\) is not available.

## 5. The non-local--local branch is closed by Torti

The old master ledger retained a flag for the noncommutative order-four
fiber \(\alpha _2\rtimes\mu _2\). In Torti's notation this is
\(G_\lambda=\alpha_p\rtimes_\lambda\mu_{p^m}\) with
\((p,m,\lambda)=(2,1,1)\). Theorems 1.3 and 4.3 of the peer-reviewed 2025
paper state that every finite flat deformation over a local Artin ring of
positive residue characteristic of every such \(G_\lambda\) is killed by
its order. Specializing the parameters therefore kills every rank-four
deformation of \(\alpha _2\rtimes\mu _2\). The same conclusion for a form
which splits after a faithfully flat residue extension descends fpqc-locally.

There is a typographical overclaim in the paper's final computation: it says
that the classified deformation is killed by \(p^m\), which is false for
\((p,m)=(2,1)\). This does not invalidate the killed-by-order conclusion in
the rank-four case. The classified law specializes to

\[
 (x,y)\circ(x',y')=
 ((1+y)x'+x+a\pi xx',\;y+y'+yy'),\qquad x^2=y^2=0.
\]

Thus

\[
 [2](x,y)=(xy,0),\qquad [4](x,y)=[2](xy,0)=(0,0).
\]

One must not quote the false stronger assertion \([2]=e\).

Combined with Deligne's commutative theorem and the block-rigidity results
in THEORY_order4.md, a counterexample may now be sought entirely on the
local--local fibers:

\[
  \alpha_2^2,\qquad W_2[F],\qquad
  k[t]/t^4\text{ with the four }(c_1,c_4)\text{ coproducts}.
\]

This use of Torti is a literature reduction, not a new proof of his
classification theorem. The peer-reviewed primary source is
<https://doi.org/10.4171/DM/1055>; the preprint is
<https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.12129>.

## 6. A direct search replacing the two stretched timeouts

The ordinary and 7200-second \(S'\) searches both left exactly

    s_f2 / t4_11 / i1
    q00  / t4_11 / i1

unknown. Repeating that encoding is low value. The minimal-Gorenstein-base
reduction asks instead for all length-seven Gorenstein socle lifts of these
two length-six quotients. After choosing the socle generator \(z=x^5\), the
nondegenerate socle pairing gives exactly six coordinate presentations.

For

\[
 Q_s=\mathbf F_2[x,y]/(x^5,xy,y^2),
\]

the four lifts are

\[
 R_{\alpha,\gamma}=
 \mathbf Z[x,y,z]/
 (z-x^5,\;xz,\;yz,\;z^2,\;xy-\alpha z,\;y^2-z,\;2-\gamma z),
 \qquad \alpha,\gamma\in\mathbf F_2.                 \tag{6.1}
\]

The socle pairing from \(\langle x^4,y\rangle\) to the dual of
\(\mathfrak m/\mathfrak m^2=\langle x,y\rangle\) has matrix

\[
  \begin{pmatrix}1&0\\ \alpha&\beta\end{pmatrix},
\]

so Gorensteinness forces \(\beta=1\), while \(\alpha\) and the characteristic
parameter \(\gamma\) are free.

For

\[
 Q_q=\mathbf Z[x]/(x^5,2x,4),
\]

the two lifts are

\[
 R_\alpha=
 \mathbf Z[x,z]/(z-x^5,\;xz,\;z^2,\;2x-\alpha z,\;4-z),
 \qquad \alpha\in\mathbf F_2.                       \tag{6.2}
\]

Here the socle pairing in the bases \(\langle x^4,2\rangle\) and
\(\langle x,2\rangle\) is

\[
  \begin{pmatrix}1&0\\ \alpha&\gamma\end{pmatrix};
\]

Gorensteinness forces \(\gamma=1\).

On a \(t^4\) fiber every rank-four augmented algebra lift is monogenic:

\[
 A=R[T]/(T^4-aT-bT^2-dT^3),\qquad a,b,d\in\mathfrak m_R.
\]

Write

\[
 \Delta T=T\otimes1+1\otimes T+
           \sum_{1\le i,j\le3}c_{ij}T^i\otimes T^j,
\]

pinning the residues of \(c_{ij}\) to the \(t^4_{11}\) normal form. It is
enough to impose relation stability and coassociativity on \(T\); counitality
is built in, and a finite free bialgebra whose special fiber is Hopf has an
antipode by lifting convolution invertibility. Compute

\[
 q(T)=\mu\Delta(T),\qquad p(T)=q(q(T)),
\]

and ask directly for \(p(T)\ne0\). Since \(T\) generates \(A\), this is
exactly \([4]\ne e\), not the stronger condition \(S'\ne0\).

## 7. What would finish the rank-four proof

There are now two particularly sharp targets.

1. **Conormal target:** prove \((1+\delta)J=0\) for a rank-four commutative
   coordinate Hopf algebra, where \(J\) is the ideal of the 2-torsion
   equalizer. The preceding argument already gives
   \((1+\delta)J^2=0\).
2. **Monogenic local target:** in the \(t^4\) branch, use relation stability
   to rule out the remaining linear class in
   \((1+\delta)(J/J^2)\). The first quartic-relation layer, trace-depth, and
   relative-top restrictions in MONOGENIC_T4_RELATION_STABILITY_2026-07-10.md
   are calibrated precisely for this step.

An UNSAT result for all six direct searches would close the two currently
hard stretched quotient rows under the stated structural reduction. It
would still not prove arbitrary-depth rank four. A SAT result would be an
actual non-killed-by-four bialgebra candidate, but it would require an
independent full axiom and antipode evaluation before being called a
counterexample.
