# The stretched length-six quotient: a complete eleven-ring list

Date: 2026-07-10

## Statement

Let \((Q,\mathfrak m,k)\) be a commutative Artin local ring with
\(k=\mathbf F _2\), Hilbert function

\[
 H_Q=(1,2,1,1,1),
\]

and \(\dim_k\operatorname {Soc}(Q)=2\).  Then \(Q\) is isomorphic to
exactly one of the following eleven rings:

1. the seven split rings
   \[
   B(P)=P\times_{\mathbf F _2}\mathbf F _2[y]/y^2
       =P[y]/(y^2,\mathfrak m_Py),
   \]
   where \(P\) runs through the seven principal length-five local rings
   with residue field \(\mathbf F _2\);
2. the four rings
   \[
   Q_{a,b}=\mathbf Z[x]/
   (x^5,\ 2x-a x^3-bx^4,\ 4-a x^4),
   \qquad (a,b)\in\mathbf F _2^2.
   \]

In particular, these eleven rings are a complete upper list for the socle
quotient of a length-seven Gorenstein local ring in the
\((1,2,1,1,1,1)\) stratum.  No Gorenstein-liftability assumption is used in
the classification.

## 1. The intrinsic hidden line and the active chain

Since the layers \(\mathfrak m^i/\mathfrak m^{i+1}\), \(2\leq i\leq4\),
are one-dimensional and nonzero,

\[
 \operatorname {Soc}(Q)\cap\mathfrak m^2=\mathfrak m^4.
\]

Indeed, if a socle element generated
\(\mathfrak m^j/\mathfrak m^{j+1}\) for \(j=2\) or \(3\), multiplying
\(\mathfrak m^j=Qz+\mathfrak m^{j+1}\) by \(\mathfrak m\) would give
\(\mathfrak m^{j+1}=\mathfrak m^{j+2}\), contrary to the Hilbert function.
The reverse inclusion for \(j=4\) follows from \(\mathfrak m^5=0\).

Consequently the image

\[
 K=(\operatorname {Soc}(Q)+\mathfrak m^2)/\mathfrak m^2
       \subset V=\mathfrak m/\mathfrak m^2
\]

is an intrinsic line.  Choose \(y\in\operatorname {Soc}(Q)\setminus
\mathfrak m^2\) spanning it, and choose \(x\) whose tangent class is not in
\(K\).  Then

\[
 y\mathfrak m=0,\qquad
 \mathfrak m^i=(x^i)\quad(2\leq i\leq4),\qquad
 x^4\ne0,\quad x^5=0.
\]

The first assertion is the definition of \(y\); the rest follows from
\(\mathfrak m=(x,y)\) and the strict one-dimensional layers.  In
particular every element of \(\mathfrak m^2\) has a unique filtered-digit
expression

\[
 a_2x^2+a_3x^3+a_4x^4,\qquad a_i\in\mathbf F _2.
\]

This accounts for all multiplication parameters: after choosing the actual
socle lift \(y\), every product involving \(y\) vanishes and every remaining
product is a power of the single active element \(x\).  The only filtered
carry still to locate is the intrinsic element \(2\).

## 2. The three possible tangent positions of \(2\)

There are three cases for the image \([2]\in V\).

### 2.1. \([2]\notin K\)

Take \(x=2\).  Its powers generate all four nonzero chain layers, so
\(2^4\ne0\) and \(2^5=0\).  Thus the image of \(\mathbf Z\) is
\(\mathbf Z/32\).  It intersects \(\mathbf F _2y\) trivially (their tangent
lines differ), and hence

\[
 Q\simeq B(\mathbf Z/32).
\]

This also covers the apparent tangent position \([2]=[x]+[y]\): over
\(\mathbf F _2\), any nonzero tangent vector outside \(K\) can itself be
chosen as the active generator.

### 2.2. \([2]=0\)

Put \(P=\mathbf Z[x]\subset Q\).  Reduction gives a surjection
\(P\to Q/(y)\), whose target is a principal length-five ring.  Its kernel is
zero.  Indeed, if a polynomial in \(x\) were equal to \(y\), then modulo
\(\mathfrak m^2\) its even constant term would vanish because
\(2\in\mathfrak m^2\), and its linear term would lie in \(k[x]\), never in
the distinct line \(k[y]\).  Hence \(|P|=32\) and

\[
 Q=P\oplus\mathbf F _2y\simeq B(P).
\]

Together with the preceding case, this produces \(B(P)\) for all seven
principal length-five rings.

### 2.3. \([2]=K\)

Filtered digits give

\[
 2=y+a x^2+b x^3+c x^4,qquad a,b,c\in\mathbf F _2.
\]

Replacing \(y\) by \(y+cx^4\) preserves the socle condition and removes
\(c\).  Since \(yx=0\), multiplication by \(x\) gives

\[
 2x=a x^3+b x^4.
\]

Multiplying the normalized equality for \(2\) by \(2\), and using the last
relation, gives

\[
 4=a x^4.
\]

Finally \(y=2-a x^2-bx^3\), so \(Q\) is generated by \(x\) over
\(\mathbf Z\), and the displayed relations give a surjection
\(Q_{a,b}\twoheadrightarrow Q\).  The relations give the following
additive normal forms, all of order at most \(64\):

| \((a,b)\) | characteristic | additive group |
|---|---:|---|
| \((0,0)\) | 4 | \(C_4\oplus C_2^4\) |
| \((0,1)\) | 4 | \(C_4^2\oplus C_2^2\) |
| \((1,0)\) | 8 | \(C_8\oplus C_4\oplus C_2\) |
| \((1,1)\) | 8 | \(C_8\oplus C_4\oplus C_2\) |

For \(a=1\), one may use the additive generators
\(1,x,s=x^2-2\), of orders at most \(8,4,2\); the relations are
\(2s=0\) and \(xs=4b\).  Since the target already has order \(64\), each
surjection is an isomorphism.  Conversely these four presentations have
the stated Hilbert function and socle \(\langle x^4,y\rangle\), where
\(y=2-a x^2-bx^3\).

## 3. Changes of the adapted generators

The four pairs \((a,b)\) do not hide any further filtered parameter.  Any
other adapted generators have the form

\[
 \widetilde x=x+\epsilon y+\alpha x^2+\beta x^3+\gamma x^4,
 \qquad
 \widetilde y=y+\delta x^4,
\]

with coefficients in \(\mathbf F _2\).  In every \(Q_{a,b}\),
\(2x^3=2x^4=0\), and hence

\[
 \widetilde x^2=x^2+\alpha x^4,qquad
 \widetilde x^3=x^3+\alpha x^4,qquad
 \widetilde x^4=x^4.
\]

Writing \(2\) in the new generators introduces only the top coefficient
\(\delta+\alpha(a+b)\).  Requiring the same normalization (zero
\(\widetilde x^4\)-coefficient) fixes
\(\delta=\alpha(a+b)\), while leaving \(a\) and \(b\) unchanged.  Since a
ring isomorphism preserves the filtration, the intrinsic line \(K\), and
the element \(2\), this proves that the four \(Q_{a,b}\) are pairwise
nonisomorphic.  (Pairwise nonisomorphism is needed only for the exact class
count; retaining all four was already safe for a negative sweep before this
reduction.)

The split and hidden-carry cases cannot be isomorphic because the three
positions \([2]=0\), \([2]=K\), and \([2]\notin K\) are intrinsic.  Among
the split rings, quotienting by either socle line outside \(\mathfrak m^2\)
recovers the same principal ring \(P\), so the seven split classes are also
distinct once the principal rings are.

## 4. The seven principal length-five rings and their carries

For completeness, let \(P\) be principal of length five and let \(t\) be a
uniformizer.  The order of \(2\) in the filtration is
\(e=1,2,3,4\), or \(2=0\).

* If \(e=1\), then \(P=\mathbf Z/32\); if \(2=0\), then
  \(P=\mathbf F _2[t]/t^5\).
* If \(e=4\), scaling is invisible in the socle and gives \(2=t^4\).
* If \(e=3\), initially \(2=t^3+ct^4\).  The change
  \(t\mapsto t+ct^2\) removes \(c\).
* If \(e=2\), every presentation normalizes to
  \[
  P_{c,d}=\mathbf Z/8[t]/
       (4t,\ t^2-2-2ct-4d),\qquad c,d\in\mathbf F _2.
  \]
  Explicitly, starting from
  \(2=t^2+\alpha t^3+\beta t^4\), multiplication gives
  \(4=t^4\), \(4t=0\), and
  \(t^3=2t-\alpha t^4\); substitution yields the displayed form with
  \(c=\alpha\) and \(d=\alpha+\beta\).
  For a general uniformizer
  \(q=t+A t^2+B t^3+C t^4\), one computes
  \[
  c'=c,\qquad d'=d+A(1+c).
  \]
  Thus \(P_{0,0}\simeq P_{0,1}\), while
  \(P_{1,0}\) and \(P_{1,1}\) give two further classes.

The resulting seven classes, with the extra hidden \(C_2y\) already added,
have the following coarse invariants:

| principal factor \(P\) | characteristic of \(B(P)\) | additive group of \(B(P)\) |
|---|---:|---|
| \(\mathbf F _2[t]/t^5\) | 2 | \(C_2^6\) |
| \(\mathbf Z/32\) | 32 | \(C_{32}\oplus C_2\) |
| the three \(P_{c,d}\)-classes | 8 | \(C_8\oplus C_4\oplus C_2\) |
| \(\mathbf Z/4[t]/(t^3-2,t^5)\) | 4 | \(C_4^2\oplus C_2^2\) |
| \(\mathbf Z/4[t]/(t^4-2,t^5)\) | 4 | \(C_4\oplus C_2^4\) |

This explicitly accounts for every characteristic and additive-group
possibility in the eleven-ring list.

## 5. Why liftability is not needed for a negative sweep

If \(Q=R/\operatorname {Soc}(R)\) for a length-seven Gorenstein ring in
this stratum, the general type lemma forces \(Q\) to satisfy the hypotheses
above.  Hence it belongs to the eleven-ring list.  It is not necessary to
decide which of the eleven rings actually admit such a Gorenstein lift in
order to prove a **negative** result: universal \(S'\) on the entire upper
list proves it on the liftable subset.  Liftability becomes necessary only
if a positive \(S'\)-failure is found and one wants to turn that quotient
seed into a length-seven counterexample.

Thus a timeout, OOM, or incomplete row remains inconclusive, but a completed
negative eleven-ring sweep is logically sufficient without a separate
Gorenstein-lift enumeration.
