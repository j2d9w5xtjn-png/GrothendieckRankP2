# Block rigidity for the `alpha_2 x mu_2` deformation locus

## Statement

Let `(R,m,k)` be an Artin local ring with `char(k)=2`, and let `G/R` be a finite locally free group scheme of degree four. No commutativity of `G` is assumed. If

```text
G_k is isomorphic to alpha_2 x mu_2,
```

then `G` is commutative. Consequently `[4]_G` is the constant identity map.

The argument is entirely algebraic and is independent of the length of `R`, the ramification of `2`, and the deformation coordinates used in the upstream searches.

## 1. Finite duality before commutativity

Write

```text
A = O(G),
H = A^vee = Hom_R(A,R).
```

The coordinate algebra `A` is a commutative finite free `R`-algebra, but its coproduct need not be cocommutative. Dualizing the coproduct gives an associative unital multiplication on `H`. Thus `H` is a finite free rank-four `R`-algebra even when `G` is noncommutative.

Because `A` is finite projective, finite duality commutes with base change:

```text
H tensor_R k  =  (A tensor_R k)^vee.
```

The closed fiber is commutative, so its finite dual is its usual Cartier-dual coordinate algebra. In characteristic two,

```text
alpha_2^D = alpha_2,
mu_2^D = (Z/2Z)_k.
```

Therefore

```text
H_k
 = O((alpha_2 x mu_2)^D)
 = O(alpha_2 x (Z/2Z)_k)
 = k[u]/(u^2) tensor_k (k x k)
 = k[u]/(u^2) x k[u]/(u^2).
```

The decisive feature is not merely commutativity of the fiber: it is the decomposition into two central blocks, each of dimension two.

## 2. Central blocks cannot couple through a flat nilpotent deformation

### Lemma (two-block Peirce rigidity)

Let `(R,m,k)` be Artin local, and let `B` be an associative unital `R`-algebra finite free of rank four. Suppose

```text
B_k = B_0 x B_1
```

with `dim_k(B_0)=dim_k(B_1)=2`. Then `B` is commutative.

### Proof

Let `e_bar=(1,0)` in `B_k`. The ideal `mB` is nilpotent, so idempotents lift modulo `mB`; choose an idempotent `e in B` reducing to `e_bar`, and put `f=1-e`.

Consider the Peirce modules

```text
eBe,  eBf,  fBe,  fBf.
```

The map

```text
B -> B,  b |-> e b f
```

is an idempotent `R`-linear endomorphism, because applying it twice again gives `ebf`. Hence `eBf` is a finite projective direct summand of the free `R`-module `B`. Its closed fiber is

```text
(eBf) tensor_R k = e_bar B_k (1-e_bar) = 0,
```

because `e_bar` is central in the product algebra `B_k`. Nakayama's lemma gives `eBf=0`. The same argument gives `fBe=0`.

Thus `e` commutes with every element of `B`, and

```text
B = eBe x fBf.
```

Both factors are finite free of rank two: their closed fibers have dimension two, and they are projective over the local ring `R`.

It remains to observe that any unital rank-two algebra over a commutative local ring is commutative. The unit is unimodular and extends to a basis `(1,x)`. Every element is of the form `a+bx`, and two such elements commute because the algebra is generated by the single element `x` over the central image of `R`.

Therefore both blocks, and hence `B`, are commutative. QED.

### Generalization

The same proof works for any finite free `R`-algebra whose closed fiber is a product of central blocks of dimension at most two. Lift the block idempotents, kill all off-diagonal Peirce modules by projectivity plus Nakayama, and note that each lifted block is commutative.

## 3. Application to the group scheme

Apply the lemma to `B=H`. The calculation in Section 1 gives the required two-block closed fiber, so `H` is commutative.

Multiplication on `H=A^vee` is dual to the coproduct on `A`. Since `A` is finite projective,

```text
H commutative
iff multiplication_H = multiplication_H after swapping factors
iff Delta_A = swap composed with Delta_A
iff G is commutative.
```

Thus every deformation in this locus is commutative.

## 4. Why a commutative finite flat group of degree n is killed by n

Here is a self-contained norm-cycle formulation of the standard Deligne theorem.

Let `G=Spec(A)` be finite locally free and commutative of degree `n` over `S=Spec(R)`. Put

```text
TS_R^n(A) = (A tensor_R ... tensor_R A)^{S_n}.
```

For a finite locally free algebra `A`, the determinant norm `N_{A/R}(a)=det(m_a)` determines the Ferrand homomorphism

```text
Phi_A : TS_R^n(A) -> R,
```

characterized by `Phi_A(a tensor ... tensor a)=N_{A/R}(a)`. It is invariant under every `R`-algebra automorphism of `A`, because multiplication matrices are conjugated.

The `n`-fold product map `G^n -> G` is invariant under permutations because `G` is commutative. On coordinate rings, its pullback therefore lands in `TS_R^n(A)`. Composing with `Phi_A` gives a canonical point `c in G(R)`, the product of the norm cycle.

After any base change `T -> S` and for any `g in G(T)`, translation by `g` is an automorphism of `G_T`, so it fixes the norm cycle. Translating all `n` entries multiplies their product by `g^n`. Therefore

```text
c_T = g^n c_T.
```

Cancel `c_T` in the group. Then `g^n=e` for every `T` and every `g in G(T)`. By Yoneda, `[n]_G` is the constant identity morphism.

Taking `n=4` proves the desired annihilation on the `alpha_2 x mu_2` locus.

## 5. Rank-two filtration

After commutativity is known, the finite dual is the coordinate algebra of the Cartier dual `G^D`. The lifted central idempotent splits the underlying finite flat scheme `G^D` into two open-and-closed rank-two pieces.

Choose the piece `K` containing the identity. It is a subgroup:

- the special fiber of `K x K` is connected and its product lies in the identity component;
- the preimage of the other component under multiplication is open and closed with empty special fiber, hence empty over the Artin local base;
- inversion is handled similarly.

The fppf quotient `G^D/K` is finite locally free of rank two. Cartier duality gives an exact sequence

```text
0 -> (G^D/K)^D -> G -> K^D -> 0,
```

with rank-two kernel and rank-two quotient.

Thus the filtration sought in several upstream construction attempts exists automatically on this locus, but it is obtained after the block-rigidity argument rather than by solving subgroup-lifting equations directly.

## 6. Square-zero consistency check

Let `J` be an ideal of a ring `R` with `J^2=0`, and let `X=Spec(A)` be an affine pointed flat `R`-scheme with augmentation ideal `I`. If a pointed endomorphism `f` is the constant identity map modulo `J`, then `f^2` is already constant over `R`.

Indeed, the point splits `A=R direct-sum I`. Triviality modulo `J` gives

```text
f^*(I) subset I intersect JA = JI.
```

Therefore

```text
(f^2)^*(I) subset J f^*(I) subset J^2 I = 0.
```

For the square map this says: a square-zero deformation of an exponent-two group has exponent at most four, without any commutativity assumption.

## 7. Exact scope and limitation

This note proves the theorem on the specified special-fiber locus. It does not prove that every unresolved rank-four group scheme has this fiber. The upstream canonical handoff explicitly retains other killed-by-two fibers and arbitrary-depth noncommutative branches. Therefore the correct consequence is:

> The most credible mixed-characteristic `alpha_2 x mu_2` counterexample locus is eliminated uniformly.

A full rank-four theorem still requires either:

1. a valid case-exhaustion theorem reducing all remaining branches to loci already known, or
2. a new uniform argument covering the residual local dual-algebra blocks of rank four.
