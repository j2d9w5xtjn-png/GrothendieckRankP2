## Six-lift completeness

For the quotient
\(\mathbf F_2[x,y]/(x^5,xy,y^2)\), a one-dimensional Gorenstein socle
extension has \(x^5=z\) after normalization and only

\[
 2=\gamma z,\qquad xy=\alpha z,\qquad y^2=\beta z.
\]

The socle/cotangent pairing
\(\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\ \alpha&\beta\end{pmatrix}\) forces \(\beta=1\).
This gives four presentations and two isomorphism classes; the change
\(y\mapsto y+\alpha x^4\) removes \(\alpha\).

For \(\mathbf Z[x]/(x^5,2x,4)\), the only carries are

\[
 x^5=z,\qquad 2x=\alpha z,\qquad4=\gamma z.
\]

The pairing
\(\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\ \alpha&\gamma\end{pmatrix}\) forces \(\gamma=1\).
This gives two presentations of one isomorphism class, with
\(x\mapsto x+2\) toggling \(\alpha\). No extension parameter is omitted:
the displayed quotient ideals are generated by exactly these relations and
\(z\mathfrak m=0\).

## Exact query and controls

The driver computes \(q=\mu\Delta\) and \(q(q(T))\) correctly. Since \(T\)
generates \(A\) and \(\varepsilon(T)=0\),

\[
 q(q(T))\ne0\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad[4]^\#\ne\eta\varepsilon.
\]

The disequality is an exact disjunction over all four \(A\)-coordinates and
all cyclic coordinates of the base ring. H0 SAT excludes vacuity. Removing

representatives; this is a cross-tactic check within Z3, not an independent
proof certificate.
