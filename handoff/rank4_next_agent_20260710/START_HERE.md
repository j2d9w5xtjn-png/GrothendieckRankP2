## Universal exponent-8 proof in one page

Let \(T\) be universal right translation, with
\(\chi=\operatorname{Tr}(T)\) and \(\delta=\det T\). The established
rank-four characteristic-polynomial identities give

\[
 c_2=2\chi-1-\delta,\qquad
 D=(1+\delta)(q-e).
\]

The regular integral identity gives \(\chi^2=4\chi\). Coassociativity gives
\((\operatorname{id}\otimes q)(T)=T^2\), hence

\[
 q(\chi)=\operatorname{Tr}(T^2)
 =\chi^2-2c_2=2(1+\delta).
\]

For \(c=\chi-4\) and \(a\in\ker\varepsilon\),
\(ac=-4a\). Applying the algebra map \(q\) yields

\[
 2(\delta-1)q(a)=-4q(a),
\]

so \(2(1+\delta)q(a)=0\), i.e. \(2D=0\). Since
\(q(\delta)=\delta^2=1\), one has \(qD=2D=0\). Therefore

\[
 [8]^\#=q^3=q(e+D)=e.
\]

No division by two and no commutativity of the group law is used.
