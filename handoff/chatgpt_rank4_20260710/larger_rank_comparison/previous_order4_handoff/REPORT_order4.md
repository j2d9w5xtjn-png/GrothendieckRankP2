# Grothendieck's question for group schemes of order 4: report

**Date:** 2026-07-08.
**Question** (Grothendieck, SGA 3 VIII 7.3.1; Tate–Oort 1970): *is every finite locally
free group scheme of order $n$ killed by $n$, i.e. is the $n$-th power map
$[n]\colon g\mapsto g^n$ the identity section?* Known: yes if $G$ is commutative
(Deligne), yes over reduced bases (SGA 3), yes over Artin local $R$ with
$m^p = pm = 0$ (Schoof 2001), yes for special fiber $\alpha_p\rtimes_\lambda\mu_{p^m}$,
$\lambda\ne0$ (Schoof 2001; Torti 2024, arXiv:2411.12129). Open in general; Torti
explicitly flags mixed-characteristic deformations of commutative fibers such as
$\alpha_2\times\mu_2$ (e.g. over $\mathbb Z/8$) as the open frontier for order $p^2$.

This report documents computer-assisted **positive** results for $n=4$ over the first
genuinely open bases, extending the handoff plan `macaulay2_handoff_order4.md` (whose
two fixed-algebra branches turn out to be trivially empty; see §2).

---

## 1. Main results established

Throughout, "fiber killed by 2" means the special fiber $G_k$ satisfies $[2]_{G_k}=0$.

**Theorem A (equal characteristic, ideal-membership certificate).**
Let $k$ be **any** commutative $\mathbb F_2$-algebra, $R = k[\epsilon]/(\epsilon^3)$.
Let $A$ be a free rank-4 $R$-**bialgebra** (commutative algebra, counital coassociative
comultiplication, $\Delta$ an algebra map; **no antipode required**) whose structure
constants reduce mod $\epsilon$ to those of $k[x,y]/(x^2,y^2)$ or $k[t]/(t^4)$, and
whose fiber is killed by 2. Then $[4]^\# = \mu^{(4)}\circ\Delta^{(4)}$ kills the
augmentation ideal: $G = \operatorname{Spec} A$ is killed by 4.
*Proof:* every coefficient of $[4]^\#$ lies in the ideal generated by the coefficient
equations of the bialgebra axioms and the fiber condition — Gröbner ideal membership
over $\mathbb F_2$ (`scripts/gensearch.m2`), with explicit cofactor certificates
verified by direct multiplication (`scripts/certs.m2`). Ideal membership makes the
statement an identity valid over every $\mathbb F_2$-algebra parameter ring at once.

**Theorem B (mixed characteristic, SAT certificates).**
There is **no** free rank-4 bialgebra with fiber killed by 2 and $[4]^\#\neq 0$ over
any of the rings
$$\mathbb Z/8,\qquad \mathbb Z[\pi]/(\pi^2-2,\pi^3),\qquad W(\mathbb F_4)/8,\qquad
W(\mathbb F_4)[\pi]/(\pi^2-2,\pi^3),\qquad \mathbb F_4[\epsilon]/(\epsilon^3),
\qquad W(\mathbb F_8)/8,$$
for either fiber algebra shape. (Z3 bitvector UNSAT, exact arithmetic:
`scripts/z8sat.py`, `scripts/order4sat.py`, `scripts/order4sat_f8.py`. The
$\mathbb F_2[\epsilon]/\epsilon^3$ case was re-proved by SAT as a cross-validation
of Theorem A — two independent toolchains agree.) The base-ring arithmetic
underlying every one of these certificates is itself machine-validated; see §3.1.

**Corollary C.** Every finite locally free group scheme of order 4 over **any Artin
local ring of length $\le 3$ with residue field $\mathbb F_2$ or $\mathbb F_4$** is
killed by 4. In particular this holds over $\mathbb Z/8$ and over
$\mathbb F_2[\epsilon]/(\epsilon^3)$, and it settles (for these bases) the
deformations of $\alpha_2\times\mu_2$ singled out as open by Torti.

*Proof of Corollary C from A + B and standard theory.* Let $(R,m,k)$ be such a ring.
Length $\le 2$: either $R$ is a field (known: over a field $I^4=0$ for a rank-4 local
algebra, and $[4]^\#(I)\subseteq I^4$; étale part by Lagrange) or $m^2=0=2m$, which is
Schoof 2001. Length 3 with $m^2 = 0$: again Schoof. Length 3 with $m^2\neq 0$:
$\dim_k m/m^2 = 1$, so $m$ is principal and $R$ is one of $k[\epsilon]/\epsilon^3$,
$W(k)/8$, $\mathbb Z[\pi]/(\pi^2-2u,\pi^3)\otimes W(k)$; and since $4=0$ in the
ramified ring, $\pi^2 = 2u \sim 2$ after a unit change of $\pi$ (Frobenius is
surjective on $\mathbb F_4^\times$), leaving exactly the listed rings. Now take $G$
of order 4 over $R$:
1. If $G_k$ has a nontrivial étale quotient: the connected-étale sequence over the
   henselian local $R$ gives $1\to G^0\to G\to G^{et}\to 1$ with orders $(1,4)$,
   $(2,2)$ or $G$ local. Étale $G$ of order 4 is commutative (groups of order 4 are
   abelian) — Deligne. Orders $(2,2)$: both $G^0$ and $G^{et}$ have order 2, hence are
   killed by 2 (Tate–Oort); for a $T$-point $g$, $g^2\in G^0(T)$, so $g^4 = e$.
2. $G$ local, $G_k$ killed by 2: since $k$ is perfect, $A_k \cong k[x,y]/(x^2,y^2)$
   or $k[t]/(t^4)$ (structure theorem for finite connected group schemes over perfect
   fields); lifting a fiber basis to $A$ puts the structure constants in the ansatz of
   Theorems A/B. Killed by 4.
3. $G$ local, $G_k$ **not** killed by 2: after the faithfully flat base change
   $R\to R' = R\,\widehat\otimes\, W(\bar{\mathbb F}_2)$ (Artin local, residue field
   $\bar{\mathbb F}_2$; being killed by 4 descends), Schoof's classification gives
   $G_{\bar k}\cong \mu_4$ or $\alpha_2\rtimes\mu_2$. For $\mu_4$: multiplicative-type
   group schemes are rigid, so $G_{R'}\cong\mu_4$-form, commutative — Deligne. For
   $\alpha_2\rtimes\mu_2$: Schoof 2001, Thm. 1.2 (any local Artin base). $\square$

**Theorem F (minimal axioms, ideal-membership certificate; session 4).**
Let $k$ be **any** commutative $\mathbb F_2$-algebra, $R = k[\epsilon]/(\epsilon^3)$.
Let $A$ be a free rank-4 **commutative associative** counital $R$-algebra whose
structure constants reduce mod $\epsilon$ to those of $k[x,y]/(x^2,y^2)$ **or**
$k[t]/(t^4)$, and let $\Delta: A\to A\otimes_R A$ be **any multiplicative,
counitally normalized linear map** — *no coassociativity, no fiber condition, no
antipode*. Then $(\mu\circ\Delta)^2 = \eta\varepsilon$: $A$ is killed by 4.
*Proof:* all $[4]^\#$ coefficients (21 for the $xy$ fiber, 24 for the $t^4$ fiber)
lie in the ideal generated by the associativity and $\Delta$-multiplicativity
equations alone — Gröbner ideal membership over $\mathbb F_2$
(`scripts/minassoc.m2`, `minassoc.log`, `DONE`: $xy$ fiber closed at
`DegreeLimit` 5, $t^4$ fiber at `DegreeLimit` 6; validation gates 16.1/16.2
pass, printed at the top of the log). This is the ideal-membership upgrade of the
ablation-minimal axiom set $\{$M, A$\}$ (`ablate2.log`) and proves the **Target
Lemma** of `THEORY_order4.md` §8 for arbitrary $k$.

> **Theorem F subsumes Theorem A and Theorem D at $\epsilon^3$.** Any bialgebra
> satisfies associativity + $\Delta$-multiplicativity, so its $[4]^\#$ vanishes by F
> regardless of coassociativity or the fiber condition; membership in the smaller
> ideal (M+A equations only) is membership in every larger equation ideal. In the
> $\psi$-language of `THEORY_order4.md` §6: the identity $\psi^2=0$ needs only
> $\Delta$-multiplicativity + associativity of the deformed product.

## 2. Hand reductions (why the handoff's first two branches are empty)

For $g\in I$, counitality forces $\Delta g \equiv g\otimes 1 + 1\otimes g \bmod
I\otimes I$; applying the (algebra map) $\mu$ gives, in residue characteristic 2 with
$2=0$ in $R$:
$$\varphi := [2]^\# = \mu\circ\Delta,\qquad \varphi(I)\subseteq I^2, \qquad
\varphi \text{ an } R\text{-algebra endomorphism}.$$
Hence $[4]^\#(I) = \varphi^2(I)\subseteq (I^2)^2 = I^4$.

* **Fixed algebra $A = R[x,y]/(x^2,y^2)$:** $\varphi(x),\varphi(y)\in R\cdot xy$ and
  $\varphi(xy) = \varphi(x)\varphi(y)\in(xy)^2 = 0$, so $[4]^\# = 0$ identically, for
  any $\Delta$ and any base — no Hopf axioms needed. Likewise for $A = R[t]/(t^4)$:
  $\varphi(t)\in(t^2)$, $\varphi(t^2)\in (t^4) = 0$. **Both fixed-algebra branches of
  the handoff are vacuously empty**; all content lies in deformations of the algebra
  structure (handoff §18–19), which is what we computed.

* **A priori bound over $m^3=0$:** fiber killed by 2 means $\varphi(I)\subseteq
  I^2\cap mA$; writing $\varphi(x) = \epsilon w$ one gets $\varphi^2(I)\subseteq
  \epsilon^2 I$ and $\varphi^3(I) = 0$: **$[8]=0$ always** over such bases; the entire
  question for $[4]$ is whether the top layer $\epsilon^2 I$ is reachable. The
  computations show the bialgebra axioms forbid it.

* **Over a field:** $I^4 = 0$ for a rank-4 local algebra, so $[4]^\# = 0$; combined
  with the étale cases, order 4 over fields is immediate (consistent with SGA 3).

## 3. Methodology and validation

Structure-constant formulation (handoff §18): $A = R\cdot 1\oplus Re_1\oplus Re_2
\oplus Re_3$, $e_ie_j = \sum_r M_{ijr}e_r$ with $M \equiv$ (standard fiber constants)
mod $m$ and arbitrary $m$-deformation; $\Delta(e_i) = e_i\otimes 1 + 1\otimes e_i +
\sum_{j,k\ge1} c_{ijk}\, e_j\otimes e_k$ (fully general given counitality).
Equations: associativity (commutativity built in), $\Delta$ multiplicative,
coassociativity, fiber killed by 2. Optional: antipode (algebra map $S$ with both
convolution identities), noncocommutativity — **not needed**: the negative results
hold already at bialgebra level, which is strictly stronger.

* Macaulay2 (equal char): one polynomial ring over $\mathbb F_2$ with $\epsilon$-digit
  variables; equations extracted per $\epsilon$-digit; ideal membership of all
  $[4]^\#$ coefficients checked by Gröbner reduction; explicit certificates
  $b = \sum_i q_i g_i$ extracted and re-verified by plain multiplication.
* Z3 (all rings): every element of the base ring encoded exactly (bitvectors:
  $\mathbb Z/8$ as 3-bit vectors; $k[\epsilon]/\epsilon^3$ as digit triples;
  $\mathbb Z[\pi]/(\pi^2{-}2,\pi^3)$ as pairs $(a,b)\in\mathbb Z/4\times\mathbb Z/2$;
  residue field $\mathbb F_4$ via $w^2+w+1$); UNSAT = nonexistence over that exact ring.

Validations passed:
* Handoff §16.1 ($\alpha_2\rtimes\mu_2$ over $k$): all Hopf equations vanish, fiber2
  fails, $[4]^\#=0$. §16.2 (noncocommutative deformation, $a=\epsilon^2$): all
  equations vanish, fiber2 holds, $\varphi(x) = \epsilon^2xy \ne 0$, $[4]^\# = 0$.
* $\mu_4$ over $\mathbb Z/8$ (hand-computed structure constants incl. carries): all
  bialgebra equations vanish mod 8, fiber2 fails ($\varphi(t) = 2t+t^2$),
  $[4]^\# = 0$. (`scripts/z8validate.m2`)
* Cross-toolchain: $\mathbb F_2[\epsilon]/\epsilon^3$ UNSAT (Z3) $\leftrightarrow$
  ideal membership (M2).
* Sanity: each SAT level-1 instance (bialgebra + fiber2, no further constraints) is
  satisfiable, so the systems are not vacuous; $1\notin J$ in all M2 runs.

### 3.1 Validation of the base-ring arithmetic (`scripts/ringcheck.py`)

Every SAT certificate is a statement about a *ring class* (a Python object supplying
`add/sub/mul/eq0/neq0/lowzero/deform`). If a multiplication table or a `deform` is
wrong, UNSAT is vacuous. Nothing checked this until now. `ringcheck.py` validates all
**18** ring classes actually used, along three independent axes:

1. **Ring axioms** on concrete elements (exhaustive below 80 elements, sampled above):
   commutativity, associativity, distributivity, units, additive inverses.
2. **Reference cross-check** against a wholly independent implementation — plain
   integer arithmetic for $\mathbb Z/2^N$; polynomial arithmetic reduced modulo the
   defining ideal for $\mathbb Z[\pi]/(\pi^2-2,\pi^3)$ and $(\pi^2-2,\pi^4)$; monomial
   models for $\mathbb F_2[x,y]/(x^2,y^2)$ and $\mathbb F_2[x,y]/(x,y)^3$; truncated
   polynomials for $\mathbb F_2[\epsilon]/\epsilon^N$.
3. **Local-ring semantics**: $m=\{x: \texttt{lowzero}(x)\}$ is an ideal; *every element
   outside $m$ is a unit* (so $m$ is maximal and $R$ is local); $m$ is nilpotent, with
   the computed chain $|m^k|$ matching the intended length; and `deform(tag)` ranges
   over **exactly** $m$ (both inclusions decided symbolically by Z3).

All 18 pass. Sample output: $\mathbb F_2[x,y]/(x,y)^3$ has $|m^k| = [32,8,1]$
($\dim m=5$, $\dim m^2=3$, $m^3=0$, length 6) and $\mathbb Z[\pi]/(\pi^2-2,\pi^4)$ has
$|m^k| = [8,4,2,1]$ (length 4), as intended.

For the extension wrappers the check is sharper: it recomputes the minimal polynomial
of $w$ from the implemented product and verifies that *its reduction mod $m$ is
irreducible*. Since $m_{R[w]} = m_R\cdot R[w]$ by construction of `lowzero`, and $R[w]$
is free of the right rank, this certifies that the wrapper really is **the** unramified
extension (unique up to isomorphism) with residue field $\mathbb F_4$ resp. $\mathbb F_8$.

> **Correction found by this check.** The class `Ext3` in `order4sat_f8.py` was named
> and documented as $R[w]/(w^3+w+1)$, but it reduces using $w^3 = w+1$, i.e. it
> implements $R[w]/(w^3-w-1)$. Over a characteristic-2 base these coincide; over
> $\mathbb Z/8$ they do **not** ($w^3+w+1$ evaluates to $2w+2\neq0$). The mathematics is
> unaffected: $w^3-w-1$ reduces mod 2 to the irreducible $w^3+w+1$, and any monic lift
> of an irreducible polynomial presents the same unramified extension, so the ring is
> $W(\mathbb F_8)/8$ either way. The name has been corrected. Had the intended lift
> mattered, this would have been a silent error in the headline $\mathbb F_8$ result.
