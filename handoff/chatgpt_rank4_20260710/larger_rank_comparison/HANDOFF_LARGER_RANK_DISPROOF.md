# Handoff: larger-rank attempted disproof of Grothendieck's finite group scheme conjecture

Date: 2026-07-09  
Branch: try to disprove by leaving rank/order 4 and searching powers of 2, mainly ranks 8 and 16 in characteristic 2.

## Executive status

No counterexample was found.

The larger-rank direction initially looked more promising than the order-4 search because a killed-by-2 special fiber of rank `2^d` has a `d`-dimensional cotangent space.  A first-order deformation can make the comorphism of doubling

\[
\Phi=[2]^\#=\mu_A\circ \Delta_A
\]

look like

\[
\Phi=e\Psi_1+e^2\Psi_2+\cdots.
\]

If `G_0` is killed by 2 and `rank(G)=2^d`, then

\[
[2^d]^\#=\Phi^d.
\]

Over the minimal test ring `k[e]/e^{d+1}`, the only possible top contribution is

\[
e^d\Psi_1^d.
\]

So a natural disproof strategy is to find a first-order deformation whose cotangent operator `L` has `L^d != 0`.

That strategy failed in all natural rank-8 and rank-16 fibers tested.  In the best tests the bialgebra equations forced `L` to map every higher generator into the bottom generator, so `L^2=0`; hence `L^3=0` and `L^4=0` automatically.

The work below should be treated as an attempted disproof/handoff, not as a proof of the conjecture.

## Important correction for the next agent

The first-order calculation only rules out counterexamples over the minimal nilpotence length where the top term is `Psi_1^d`.

For rank 8 killed-by-2 fibers, `Phi^3 mod e^4` only sees `Psi_1^3`.  If `Psi_1^3=0`, then no counterexample exists over `k[e]/e^4` by this leading-term method.  However, longer bases such as `k[e]/e^5` can still have degree-4 terms like

\[
\Psi_1^2\Psi_2+\Psi_1\Psi_2\Psi_1+\Psi_2\Psi_1^2.
\]

So the next serious search should use longer Artin bases, not merely `e^4`, if it wants a higher Massey-type obstruction.

### 2. Rank 8 non-split Witt/Frobenius kernel `W_3[F]`

For the rank-8 fiber `W_3[F]`, the first-order equations give a 2-dimensional cotangent-symbol subspace.  With basis ordered as `(output, input)` on generators `(x0,x1,x2)`, it is generated by

\[
x_1\mapsto x_0,\qquad x_2\mapsto x_0.
\]

Thus every possible `L` has `L^2=0`, so certainly `L^3=0`.

### 3. Rank 16 non-split Witt/Frobenius kernel `W_4[F]`

\[

\qquad x_2\mapsto x_0,

### 5. Rank 8 Heisenberg / `U_3`-type fiber

I also tried a non-killed-by-2 special fiber:

\[
A_0=\mathbb F_2[x,y,z]/(x^2,y^2,z^2),
\]

with

\[
\Delta x=x\otimes1+1\otimes x,
\]

\[
\Delta y=y\otimes1+1\otimes y,
\]

\[
\Delta z=z\otimes1+1\otimes z+x\otimes y.
\]

Here `Phi_0=[2]^#` is nonzero but nilpotent, and the special fiber is killed by 4 rather than 2.  This looked like a possible route to make `[8]^#=Phi^3` nonzero under deformation.

Results:

- `rank8_heis_firstorder_phi3.py`: full first-order bialgebra deformation, derivative of `Phi^3` is zero.
- `rank8_polycheck.py`: exact fixed-algebra second-order polynomial check over `F2[e]/e^3`; all expression coefficients for `Phi^3` vanish.
- `rank8_exact_certificate.py`: fast certificate companion to `rank8_exact.py`; verifies that `Phi^3` is identically zero as a quadratic polynomial in the first-order cocycle parameters and has no linear dependence on the second-layer variable `D2`.
- `rank8_search.py` and `rank8_exact.py`: older/random/exact fixed-algebra search variants.  `rank8_exact.py` can be slow; use `rank8_exact_certificate.py` or `rank8_polycheck.py` as the cleaner exact checks.

The multiplicative case corresponds to `mu_8` with `t=T-1`; the primitive case is additive/one-generator.

These do not close the entire `mu_8` deformation space, but they make the easiest fixed-algebra route look weak.

## Structural observation: why semidirect products are poor disproof candidates

Suppose a finite flat group scheme `G` of order `2^{a+b}` has a normal subgroup `N` of order `2^a` and quotient `Q` of order `2^b`, and suppose `N` and `Q` are already killed by their orders.

Then for any point `g` of `G`, the image of `g^{2^b}` in `Q` is trivial, hence `g^{2^b}` lies in `N`; then

\[
(g^{2^b})^{2^a}=1.
\]

Therefore `g^{2^{a+b}}=1`.

So extension/semidirect-product constructions are automatically safe when their factors are safe.  This explains why many obvious matrix-group ideas are structurally unlikely to disprove the conjecture.  A genuine counterexample probably needs to avoid visible flat normal subgroups of order 2 and avoid a filtration by known-safe smaller group schemes.

## What I would try next

The first-order large-Jordan-chain idea appears blocked for the natural rank-8 and rank-16 fibers.  A next agent should probably not spend more time only checking `L^d` for split/Witt fibers unless they have a classification theorem in mind.

The most plausible next attempts are:

1. **Longer nilpotence length for rank 8 killed-by-2 fibers.**  Use `R=F2[e]/e^5` or longer so that `Phi^3` can see mixed terms involving `Psi_2`, not only `Psi_1^3`.  Start with `W_3[F]` or another non-split local-local fiber.

2. **Full multiplication and comultiplication deformation, not fixed algebra only.**  The fixed-algebra Heisenberg and `mu_8` tests are restrictive.  The true deformation functor allows both `mu_s` and `Delta_s` layers.

3. **Exploit the square-zero cotangent shape.**  The repeated result `L(x_i) in <x_0>` may be the shadow of a general obstruction.  Try to prove a theorem: for killed-by-2 local rank `2^d` bialgebra fibers of the tested type, first-order bialgebra deformations force the doubling derivative to have square zero.

4. **Search for fibers with no order-2 normal subgroup.**  Semidirect/product examples are structurally bad.  Look for genuinely indecomposable, noncommutative, local-local fibers of rank 8 or 16.

5. **Use order-4 handoff machinery on rank 8.**  The previous agents developed Macaulay2/SAT-style layer-by-layer obstruction computations.  Port the `D_s` relative-defect idea from the order-4 folder into a rank-8 `Phi^3` computation over longer Artin bases.
