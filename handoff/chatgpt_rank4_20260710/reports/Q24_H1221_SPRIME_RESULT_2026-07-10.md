# Exact S-prime result for the characteristic-16 quotient Q24

## Result

The quotient `q24` in the exact classification of length-six local rings
with Hilbert function `(1,2,2,1)` and type two does **not** furnish an
`S'`-failure.

This is a particularly relevant very ramified target.  It has characteristic
16, additive widths `(4,1,1)`, a non-elementary residual syzygy quotient
`C4 x C2`, and a verified length-seven Gorenstein socle lift.  In the
normalized adapted presentation its characteristic carries are recorded as

```text
(2, 2x, 2y, 2a, 2b) = (x, a, 0, u, 0).
```

All six rational `xy` fibers and all four rational `t4` normal forms were
tested.  Five rows were H0-vacuous:

- `xy/a2a2`, `xy/W2F`, and `xy/mu2a2`;
- `t4/00` and `t4/10`.

The remaining five rows had H0 and S1 satisfiable:

- `xy/mu2mu2`;
- `xy/mu2mu2_unipotent`;
- `xy/mu2mu2_irreducible`;
- `t4/01`;
- `t4/11`.

For every nonvacuous row, all three exact coordinate-failure formulas were
UNSAT.  Thus the terminal count is

```text
10 fiber rows = 5 H0-vacuous + 5 nonvacuous;
15/15 nonvacuous S2 coordinate queries UNSAT;
0 SAT S'-failures, 0 unknowns, 0 timeouts, 0 OOMs.
```

This is an `S'` result, not a direct computation of `[4]`.  By the minimal
Gorenstein-base reduction used in the main report, it eliminates `q24` as
the socle quotient of a first length-seven rank-four counterexample.
